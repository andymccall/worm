================================================================================
                          WORM - GAME MANUAL
================================================================================

Worm is a classic snake-style game for the Commander X16 and Neo6502 retro
computers. Guide your worm around the playfield, eat flowers to grow, avoid
spiders, and survive as long as you can!


LOADING AND RUNNING
================================================================================

Commander X16
-------------

Load the WORM.PRG file from your storage device:

    LOAD "WORM.PRG",8,1
    RUN

Or launch directly in the X16 emulator:

    x16emu -prg WORM.PRG

Neo6502
-------

Load the worm.neo file using the Neo6502 emulator or flash it to your device:

    neo worm.neo cold


MAIN MENU
================================================================================

When the game starts, a short jingle plays and the main menu appears. A small
decorative worm circles the menu options while you choose:

    S  -  Start a new game
    A  -  View the About screen (author and credits)
    D  -  Watch a demo (attract mode)
    Q  -  Quit the game

If no key is pressed for 30 seconds, the game automatically enters demo mode.


HOW TO PLAY
================================================================================

Controls
--------

    W or Up Arrow     -  Move up
    S or Down Arrow   -  Move down
    A or Left Arrow   -  Move left
    D or Right Arrow  -  Move right
    P or Space        -  Pause the game
    Q                 -  Quit to menu

The worm cannot reverse direction -- you cannot turn 180 degrees back on
yourself. For example, if you are moving right, you cannot immediately move
left.

Objective
---------

Eat as many flowers as you can without dying. Each flower you eat adds one
segment to your worm and increases your food counter. As you eat more flowers,
spiders begin to appear on the playfield, making navigation more dangerous.

The Playfield
-------------

The game is played on a bordered rectangular grid. The worm, flowers, spiders,
and life pickups all occupy individual cells on this grid.

The status bar along the top of the screen shows:

    FOOD   -  The number of flowers you have eaten.
    LIVES  -  Your remaining lives, displayed as red hearts.


GLYPHS
================================================================================

Worm (Green)
------------

Your worm is drawn in bright green. Each body segment is a rounded shape. The
worm starts with 3 segments at the centre of the playfield, moving to the
right.

Flower (Yellow)
---------------

Flowers appear as a yellow four-leaf clover shape. One flower is on the
playfield at all times. When you eat it, a new one immediately spawns at a
random location.

Spider (Grey / Yellow)
----------------------

Spiders are drawn as a side-on silhouette with a head, body, abdomen, and
three pairs of legs.

    Grey spiders   -  Dangerous. Touching one costs you a life.
    Yellow spiders -  Vulnerable. Can be eaten safely. When you eat a
                      vulnerable spider, it is removed from the playfield.

See the section on Spider Behaviour below for details on when spiders appear
and become vulnerable.

Life Pickup (Red Heart)
-----------------------

Occasionally a red heart appears on the playfield. Collect it to gain an extra
life (up to a maximum of 3).


LIVES
================================================================================

You start the game with 3 lives, and can hold a maximum of 3 at any time.

You lose a life when:

    - The worm hits the border wall.
    - The worm runs into its own body.
    - The worm touches a grey (non-vulnerable) spider.

When you lose a life:

    - There is a brief pause before you respawn.
    - Your worm resets to 3 segments at the centre of the playfield.
    - All existing food, spiders, and pickups remain on the field.
    - The food counter is preserved -- your progress is not lost.

When your last life is lost, "GAME OVER!" is displayed for a few seconds
before returning to the main menu.

Extra Lives
-----------

After every 20 flowers eaten, a red heart life pickup spawns on the playfield
(provided you have fewer than 3 lives). Collect it to gain one extra life.


SPIDER BEHAVIOUR
================================================================================

Spiders are the primary hazard in Worm. They do not move -- they sit in fixed
positions on the playfield -- but they steadily fill up the grid, making
navigation increasingly difficult.

Spawning
--------

A new spider appears every 10 flowers you eat. The playfield can hold up to
8 spiders at once. If all 8 slots are full, the oldest spider is removed and
replaced with a new one in a different location.

Vulnerability
-------------

When you eat food while already at maximum lives (3), all spiders on the field
turn yellow and become vulnerable. While vulnerable:

    - Touching a spider removes it from the playfield instead of killing you.
    - Vulnerability lasts until you eat the next flower, at which point any
      remaining spiders turn grey again.

This creates a risk/reward dynamic -- you can use vulnerability windows to
clear dangerous spiders from the field, but you need to act quickly before
eating the next flower ends the window.


PAUSE AND QUIT
================================================================================

Pausing
-------

Press P or Space at any time during gameplay to pause. The screen displays
"GAME PAUSED" and the game freezes. Press any key to resume.

Quitting
--------

Press Q during gameplay to bring up a quit confirmation: "ARE YOU SURE?" with
[Y] / [N] options. Press Y to return to the main menu, or N to continue
playing.


DEMO MODE
================================================================================

Select D from the main menu (or wait 30 seconds) to watch the computer play
the game automatically. The demo follows all the same rules as normal gameplay.
Press any key to return to the main menu.


TIPS
================================================================================

    - Plan ahead. The worm moves at a steady pace -- use the time between
      moves to look ahead and find a safe path to the flower.

    - Keep to open space. Avoid boxing yourself into corners, especially as
      spiders fill the playfield.

    - Use vulnerability wisely. When spiders turn yellow, prioritise eating
      the ones that block key routes before your next flower resets them to
      grey.

    - Watch your length. The longer your worm grows, the harder it is to
      avoid running into yourself. Stay aware of where your tail is.
